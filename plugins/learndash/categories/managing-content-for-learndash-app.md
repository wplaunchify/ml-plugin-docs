## How to Add a Blog Page and Posts

**Source:** [https://learndash.com/support/kb/learndash-app/managing-content-for-learndash-app/how-to-add-a-blog-page-and-posts/](https://learndash.com/support/kb/learndash-app/managing-content-for-learndash-app/how-to-add-a-blog-page-and-posts/)

## Introduction

Blogging is a powerful way to engage your audience, showcase your expertise, and increase retention. With the Learndash App, you can easily display blog posts inside the app for mobile-first access.

This guide walks you through adding the Blog page to your app menu, creating posts, and ensuring content displays optimally on mobile.

## Adding the Blog Page to the App Menu

To make your blog accessible, add it to the **Tab Bar** or **More Screen** in the Learndash App.

1. In your WordPress Dashboard, go to **Learndash App > Menus**.

![](https://lh7-rt.googleusercontent.com/docsz/AD_4nXd2_pU5lN8ZBErvVsTroCC12ztAG-dFSYarlarfdtv25_uxjTil7z_H9OxNKCAxtu_-1njBfalFLrbvFz9--kehmLb421bUlRqzgdV_gbeMPSJY46D60zkq2cekVZKi5T2IuUxuJg?key=RTaKBtkEfEdrfrETfaBhSw)

1. Under the **App Core** section, find **Blog** and check the box next to it.
2. Click **Add to Menu**.

![](https://lh7-rt.googleusercontent.com/docsz/AD_4nXciy29ECIGGgrP2Dg3if5bhs0XIehv-0URjePlXmwHA2ppyIcHwxstXL-eaGn1lvOH1auJvUjAaDW5Afw4aLzaF_ZPW9C74pI8QBbIDaiMM6FCG6ykeZQBXchwEsZ6h2O7iDcJz0Q?key=RTaKBtkEfEdrfrETfaBhSw)

1. Drag and drop the Blog item to adjust its order in the **Tab Bar Menu** or **More Screen Menu**.
2. Click **Save Menu** to apply changes.

Once saved, the **Blog tab** will appear in your mobile app.

## Creating a Blog Post

1. In your WordPress Dashboard, go to **Posts > Add New**.

![](https://lh7-rt.googleusercontent.com/docsz/AD_4nXfG9_L0DdsMLha7RUIVE1XVnpoQoouqZBWhhAApFj9MRzAH9kNtVvB6PW9HtIgn8IrmGWdZJfYNUG2nGIiPqW1PIEBnF-AVjiZspOBy5RmJnEoaxP6LYqqiX5o-QhSYAc_WnCeAog?key=RTaKBtkEfEdrfrETfaBhSw)

1. Enter a **Title** and write your content in the editor.
2. Use **Gutenberg Blocks** to format your post.
3. Add a **Featured Image** for better visual presentation in the Blog listing.
4. Click **Publish** when you’re done.

![](https://lh7-rt.googleusercontent.com/docsz/AD_4nXePkgjWKxt8jLr0lk3JlRknzG38C3kQ_QsVzhidfTB06oF2gBd0lxYxb5aWzfMVExoFsTZz0pUFDhAilktwy_LtzmKyycutLOIP_QUtpioL_06oyLUibLEE40NPpFrzDis1w74qfw?key=RTaKBtkEfEdrfrETfaBhSw)

## Optimizing Blog Posts for Learndash App

- If your post contains unsupported blocks, a **warning** will appear at the top of the editor.
- To provide an optimized mobile version, switch to the **App Editor** tab and add alternate content.
- Once published, the post will automatically appear inside the **Blog tab** in your Learndash App.

## Troubleshooting and FAQs

**Q: Why can’t I see the Blog page in my app?****** A: Go to **Learndash App > Menus** and confirm the Blog page has been added to either the Tab Bar or More Screen. Then, force-close and reopen the app.

**Q: Why isn’t my blog post displaying correctly in the app?****** A: Some Gutenberg blocks aren’t supported in the app. A warning will show if detected. Use the **App Editor** to input fallback content designed for mobile.

**Q: Do I need to take extra steps after publishing a post?****** A: No. Published posts automatically appear in the Blog tab of your Learndash App—no syncing or refresh needed.

**Q: Can I use Gutenberg blocks in blog posts?****** A: Yes, but only supported blocks will render properly in the app. Unsupported ones may trigger a fallback display or cause layout issues.

## How to Add H5P Interactive Content to Your Learndash App

**Source:** [https://learndash.com/support/kb/learndash-app/managing-content-for-learndash-app/how-to-add-h5p-interactive-content-to-your-learndash-app/](https://learndash.com/support/kb/learndash-app/managing-content-for-learndash-app/how-to-add-h5p-interactive-content-to-your-learndash-app/)

## Introduction

H5P enables you to create rich, interactive HTML5 content such as quizzes, flashcards, and interactive videos.

This guide walks you through installing the H5P plugin, creating content, embedding it into LearnDash lessons using the H5P App Block, and previewing it in your Learndash App.

## Install the H5P Plugin

1. In your WordPress Dashboard, go to **Plugins > Add Plugin**.

![](https://lh7-rt.googleusercontent.com/docsz/AD_4nXfU4GIsJnEAkfuI5oJzGLsqpUY5PnBhGrQuBYXGV2zy9SwuuSOBYVzlAkDQL2obvuaHarKlEhaVihPE0WXwxX5DUEIB_lEs81t93Hv9wMt-klxNoO6s5wI8eLu9ls2mls06mMfaMg?key=6MPK5BjFXdPd_-CD3JD-YQ)

1. Search for **H5P** and install the plugin developed by the **H5P Community**.

![](https://lh7-rt.googleusercontent.com/docsz/AD_4nXeuU_9GC7R8N0HwBqGH19xVvfWxNlwjnArKpfzUK4Oubjx7008I_V-ujNQ6wdKuwE3mt3lZlFO4swDV5qi9lzg-_y_lZyp0F562sY_tPct575-po9WxVtMewFeSRxCvlxR5jDBi?key=6MPK5BjFXdPd_-CD3JD-YQ)

1. Click **Activate** once installation completes.

## Create H5P Content

1. In the Dashboard, navigate to **H5P Content > Add New**.

![](https://lh7-rt.googleusercontent.com/docsz/AD_4nXe4Hikh6UWBSSY_TEBViKqHsEB1rEApVhHhDtPjRuHHP-yHoPb509qfbGPL9VLPpp1W0p4J51x4vQ2yPgjeuhYLYeSg69Kwt3tk4tz__HitDFvyRl0tWMnGkWt89i1fGQavjwZn4w?key=6MPK5BjFXdPd_-CD3JD-YQ)

1. Select a content type (e.g., **Image Pairing**) and click **Get** (first-time use) or **Use**.
2. Enter a **Title**
3. Configure the interactive fields (e.g., image pairs, quiz questions).
4. Click **Save** to create the H5P item.

## Embed H5P in a LearnDash Lesson

1. Go to **LearnDash LMS > Lessons** and edit the lesson where you want to add the H5P content.
2. Click the **+ icon** and search for the **H5P block** (with the mobile icon).
3. Insert the H5P block and select the H5P item by title from the block settings.
4. Click **Update** to save the lesson.

## Preview H5P on the Web

1. Visit the course lesson on your site’s frontend.
2. Confirm the interactive content displays and works correctly within the theme layout.

## Preview H5P in the Learndash App

1. Open the **Learndash App** on your mobile device.
2. Navigate to the course and open the lesson containing H5P content.
3. Tap the **H5P block**. It will open in a scrollable modal web view.
4. Interact with the content (e.g., match pairs or complete quizzes).
5. After finishing, close the modal and tap **Mark Complete**.

## Troubleshooting and FAQs

**Q: Why isn’t the H5P block showing up in the lesson?****** A: Make sure you’re using the **H5P Gutenberg block**, not a shortcode. Shortcodes are not supported in the Learndash App.

**Q: Why doesn’t the H5P content load in the app?****** A: Ensure the **H5P plugin** is activated and that you selected the content in the block settings. Force-close and reopen the app to refresh the cache.

**Q: Can users access H5P content offline?****** A: No. H5P content loads via a web view and requires an active internet connection.

| Login Logo | 1200 × 774 | PNG or PSD |
| Login/Registration Background | 2732 × 2732 | PNG or PSD |
| Launch Screen Background | 2732 × 2732 | PNG or PSD |
| iOS/Android App Icon | 1024 × 1024 | PNG or PSD |
| Android Adaptive Icons | 1024 × 1024 | PNG |
| Android Notification Icon | 256 × 256 | PNG |

## Learndash App Branding Guidelines

### Standard Learndash App Icon (iOS and Android)

- **Size**: 1024 × 1024 px
- **Safe Zone**: 682 × 682 px center
- **Format**: PNG (32-bit), sRGB
- **iOS**: No transparency
- **Android**: Transparency allowed

### Android Adaptive Icon

Required on Android 8+ for shape flexibility and parallax.

- **Foreground**: Transparent logo, 1024 × 1024 px
- **Background**: Opaque image, 1024 × 1024 px
- **Safe Zone**: 683 × 683 px center (170 px margin)

### Launch / Splash Screen

Displayed instantly on Learndash app startup.

- **Size**: 2732 × 2732 px
- **Safe Zone**: 1200 × 2732 px center
- **Format**: PNG (32-bit), sRGB

### Login Screen

- **Logo**: Max 200 × 150 px within background
- **Background**: 2732 × 2732 px
- **Format**: PNG (32-bit), sRGB

## Troubleshooting and FAQs

**Q: Why aren’t my images uploaded?****** A: Make sure the files are in PNG or PSD format and match the recommended dimensions. Clear cache and reload the page if needed.

**Q: Why do previews look cropped or misaligned?****** A: Ensure design elements stay within the **safe zones** (e.g., 682 × 682 px for app icons). Use the provided overlay guides and re-upload if necessary.

**Q: I lost my uploads after leaving the page.****** A: You must click **Save Changes** at the bottom after every update—uploads are discarded otherwise.

**Q: Adaptive icons don’t display correctly on my Android device.****** A: Check that both foreground and background images are 1024 × 1024 px and follow the safe zone guidelines. Your device must run Android 8.0 or later.

## How to Configure Deep Linking

**Source:** [https://learndash.com/support/kb/learndash-app/managing-content-for-learndash-app/how-to-configure-deep-linking/](https://learndash.com/support/kb/learndash-app/managing-content-for-learndash-app/how-to-configure-deep-linking/)

## Introduction

Deep linking allows you to open specific screens in your Learndash App using URLs, just like on the web. This makes it easier to send users directly to targeted content like blog posts, member profiles, in-app purchases, or settings.

This guide walks you through linking to posts, native app screens, App Pages, and  In-App Products.

## Access Deep Link Settings

1. In your WordPress Dashboard, go to **Learndash App > Menus**.

![](https://lh7-rt.googleusercontent.com/docsz/AD_4nXfUvL31xQSKVmO9L0nK4nRfaI1j9fimubZwKNfZed0uIUh8eyPSWwkoyFpxCEDUWa-6_xrNx36Qe0hA6SqwKKielZvaKnws5Maw6dbR56zgjKuOGih-BJ53JdIXSW3poLjF8kgFHA?key=WoG7OVXyGjIpDfQYVXNApQ)

1. Choose either **Tab Bar Menu** or **More Screen Menu**, depending on where you want the link to appear.
2. If you’ve made changes previously, click **Save Menu** to continue.

## Link to Posts and Pages

1. Under **Custom Links,**paste the full URL of the post, page, or custom post type archive (e.g., [https://yourdomain.com/shop](https://yourdomain.com/shop)).
2. Click **Add to Menu**.
3. Drag the new menu item into position.
4. Click **Save Menu**.

![](https://lh7-rt.googleusercontent.com/docsz/AD_4nXdQtHEWcd2JmDMrNnePxW1oiqk0tHz8IF70Z64He74OTX6UnD6JPfpGvRBhTD-b0nl9l0iY16dBB9TcmXQB4nTPkcOhg9Nxa6oJK1mxk7KtcWUOB47hPGUYxIy3LrxZyDahIJiMKg?key=WoG7OVXyGjIpDfQYVXNApQ)

Note: In the app, tapping the item will open the content in a native screen (if supported) or as a web fallback.

## Link to App Pages

1. Go to **Learndash App > App Pages** and edit the desired page.

![](https://lh7-rt.googleusercontent.com/docsz/AD_4nXcsMyq39fj8WH3tVhSk1WlJErS4aoFNulAV5eX9946smAaIo70f1Ut2Xd-H4ZOybkXscDwnkG4BIpTD3amEIUGs8nt-ZWhzjbpszZNPHumnQo6ovxN3rxA4cogDNDzxSThT8NQ9HQ?key=WoG7OVXyGjIpDfQYVXNApQ)

1. In the sidebar, expand the **App Page** tab and find the **Deep Linking** section.

![](https://lh7-rt.googleusercontent.com/docsz/AD_4nXczNKZwu_E480LW9rE9OdAh4cZ9P4aszLeQxjwZkrDPMqxo_Q9IzvwufvArVRA41UsYzFUfiCMt4CK1w2NFOxN2pSvuTAkExLUdNU5XiVENq4tXXRXP5w-5qCKjOuytPviRABFGTQ?key=WoG7OVXyGjIpDfQYVXNApQ)

1. Copy the URL (e.g., https://yourdomain.com/ldapp/page/1).
2. Use it in any menu item or inside another App Page block.

## Link to In-App Products

1. Go to **Learndash App > In-App Purchases > Products** and edit a product.
2. Scroll down to the **Deep Linking** section and copy the URL (e.g., https://yourdomain.com/ldapp/products/1).
3. Use the link in menus or buttons within App Pages.

## Link to Learndash App Screens

1. Under **Learndash App > Menus > Custom Links**, add your link. Use the following format for the URL:
https://yourdomain.com/ldapp/screen/[screen-name]/
Example: /ldapp/screen/notifications/

![](https://lh7-rt.googleusercontent.com/docsz/AD_4nXcqS7ik0BmErZIDIFuFTgkrgdI8wlqzTVaamqcXeCzV6ibqUyTQuvApEY0ZUKAmrXWC--J2D0nMMtXfml7pj0aISu5IEER8KVCU3GnQzok9f2zu1cjlY2MYvrHNgpwRa10dFKpQ?key=WoG7OVXyGjIpDfQYVXNApQ)

1. Click **Add to Menu**.
2. Click **Save Menu**.

**Supported screen slugs include:**

- courses_all
- iap_products
- And others as defined in your custom screen configuration.

## Troubleshooting and FAQs

**Q: Why isn’t my custom screen link working?****** A: Ensure you’re using the correct menu_label or screen slug from your **Custom Screens** configuration.

**Q: How do I test a deep link?****** A: Add the link to a **Custom Link** in the app menu. Save, reopen the app, and tap the item to verify navigation.

## How to Use Web Fallbacks

**Source:** [https://learndash.com/support/kb/learndash-app/managing-content-for-learndash-app/how-to-use-web-fallbacks/](https://learndash.com/support/kb/learndash-app/managing-content-for-learndash-app/how-to-use-web-fallbacks/)

## Introduction

****Web Fallbacks let you display any WordPress page or custom post type inside your LearnDash App. These pages load responsively and automatically remove your site’s header and footer, offering a native-like experience.

This guide explains how to configure Web Fallbacks, when to use them, and how they compare to native App Pages.

## What Are Web Fallbacks?

Web Fallbacks allow your existing WordPress content such as WooCommerce shops or custom archives to be embedded within the app. The content is displayed with the app’s native header and navigation, while your website’s header and footer are hidden.

## Benefits of Web Fallbacks

- **Seamless Integration** – Web content blends in with the app’s native UI.
- **Cost & Time Efficient** – Use existing web content without needing custom React Native development.
- **Supports Page Builders** – Continue designing with Gutenberg, Elementor, and other tools.

## Limitations of Web Fallbacks

- **Requires Internet** – These pages load over WiFi or mobile data and are unavailable offline.
- **Dependent on Site Speed** – May load slower than native App Pages.

## Accessing Web Fallback Settings

1. In your WordPress Dashboard, go to **LearnDash App > Menus**.
2. Choose either **Tab Bar Menu** or **More Screen Menu**.

![Screenshot of Tab Bar menu](https://learndash.com/support/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/How-to-Use-Web-Fallbacks-in-the-Learndash-App-_-1.png)

1. Click **Save Menu** if you’ve made any previous changes.

## Adding Web Fallback Pages

1. Under **App Core** or **WordPress Pages**, select a page (e.g., **Shop** or **Movies**) and click **Add to Menu**.

![screenshot of WordPress pages](https://learndash.com/support/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/How-to-Use-Web-Fallbacks-in-the-Learndash-App-_-2.png)

1. Drag the new menu item into position.
2. Click **Save Menu**.

## Previewing Web Fallback Content

1. Open the LearnDash App on your device.
2. Tap the new tab (e.g., **Shop**).
3. Your website page loads without the site’s header/footer. Only the native app header is shown.
4. Tap content like a product or article to view it.
5. Use the app’s **Back** arrow to return.

## Leveraging Deep Linking to Native Screens

1. Inside a fallback page, tap a link that leads to a native screen.
2. The app detects the URL and opens the appropriate native screen.
3. Tap **Back** to return to the web fallback view.

## What Are App Pages?

App Pages are fully native screens built using App Blocks. Unlike web fallbacks, they load instantly, work offline, and offer better performance.

## Benefits of App Pages

- **Native Experience** – Smooth navigation with faster loading.
- **Offline Availability** – Content is cached for offline access.
- **Better Performance** – No external requests; served natively in-app.

## Accessing App Pages

1. In your Dashboard, go to **LearnDash App > App Pages**.

1. Create or edit App Pages using App Blocks (denoted with a phone icon).

![Screenshot of App Pages Menu](https://learndash.com/support/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/How-to-Use-Web-Fallbacks-in-the-Learndash-App-_-3.png)

## Comparing Native Screens and Web Fallbacks

| Feature | Web Fallbacks | Native App Pages |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Setup Complexity | Easy – Use existing pages | Advanced – Requires REST API + coding |
| Performance | Slower, internet dependent | Fast and responsive |
| Offline Availability | Not supported | Fully supported |
| Visual Integration | Matches app layout, not perfect | Fully integrated |
| Page Builders | Supported (Elementor, Gutenberg) | Not applicable |

## Troubleshooting and FAQs

**Q: Why aren’t my web fallback pages showing in the app?****** A: Make sure you clicked **Save Menu**, then force-close and reopen the app.

**Q: Why is the content loading slowly?****** A: Performance depends on your website’s speed and server resources. Use caching and optimization plugins where possible.
